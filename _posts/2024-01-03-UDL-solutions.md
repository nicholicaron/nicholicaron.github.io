My attempt at an unofficial, comprehensive solution set for the book *Understanding Deep Learning* by Simon J.D. Prince.

The book covers deep learning from first principles — starting with shallow neural networks and building toward transformers, diffusion models, and reinforcement learning. Each chapter includes exercises that require working through the mathematics by hand, which is the best way I know to develop real intuition for how these systems work.

This solution set is a living document. I'm working through the chapters systematically, and solutions are added as I complete them.

## Topics Covered

- **Shallow Neural Networks** — Universal approximation, activation functions
- **Deep Networks** — Residual connections, batch normalization
- **Loss Functions** — Cross-entropy, maximum likelihood
- **Training & Optimization** — SGD, Adam, learning rate schedules
- **Convolutional Networks** — Convolution arithmetic, pooling
- **Transformers** — Self-attention, positional encoding
- **Graph Neural Networks** — Message passing, aggregation
- **Diffusion Models** — Forward/reverse process, score matching
- **Reinforcement Learning** — Policy gradients, value functions

*Solutions are being added incrementally. Check back for updates.*

**Reference:** [Understanding Deep Learning](https://udlbook.github.io/udlbook/) by Simon J.D. Prince
